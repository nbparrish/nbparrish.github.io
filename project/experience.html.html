My Experience  

 

 

 

 

Hello, my name is Nikki Parrish and I reside in North Carolina. I am a 29-year-old college student and I work full-time in the IT field. I have decided to create a website where I can discuss different self-care saving tips, and not just any tips but especially for young adults. Young adults in college, trying to start a family, and even save for the future. I believe self-care is very important in life but who says it must break the bank? Not over here it doesn’t.

Once upon a time I got my nails done biweekly. It averages about $150.00 a month and that’s not even including the pedicure. CHING CHANG. my pockets were crying. I could never imagine my life without getting these things I called my self-care and maintenance bi-weekly up keep. That was until I discover press on nails and got myself a pedicure set for Christmas. You see when going to the shop my nails would constantly break and lift which I have never experience yet with the press on nails. These things were life changing. I was able to do my own nails and toes in the comfort of my own home while sipping some inexpensive wine. It was by far game changing for me. I save probably at least $150.00 a month now that I can now use towards extra groceries thanks to this inflation.

 Do my own pedi and medi? NOT I! lol Right I said the same thing. It was so comfortable to sit back and get pamper, but I’ll rather pamper myself while saving my pockets at the SAME TIME! It does take time to get use too, but I have truly fallen in love with having my self-care days in the comfort of my own home. Nail techs touch a lot of hands and with the current virus going around you don’t have to worry about catching  it either. Another cool thing I discover that helps with stree is a personal sauna. I used to visit the sauna at least once a week now I can do it in the comfort of my own home while saying money. The last thing is working out outside, yes OUTSIDE. We need the Vitamin D and stop paying gym fees for a gym you will NEVER see.
